## Family Legacy

Bastianpillai Paul Nicholas's pioneering banking legacy in Malaya. Due to the date references he is recognised as one of the first Asian bankers in the history of British Colonial Malaya.

It was my father's Dad, Bastianpillai Paul Nicholas between the years of 1918–1920, who commenced his first monetary business providing financial services in Ipoh, Perak, Malaya.

An all consumingly passionate entrepreneur driven by a strategic vision, who single-handedly brought an idea to pass, overcoming obdurate hurdles and colonial prepotency.

My Dad (Albert Arasaratnam Nicholas) is positioned in the centre of many family photographs. He was one of the Directors and the Manager of Oriental Bank of Malaya Ltd-Klang.

## Lead Kindly Light

John Henry Newman, who was a young English Priest, wrote the Hymn *Lead Kindly Light* when he was afflicted with a serious illness whilst staying in the island of Sicily. He was far from home and really believed he was facing death.

In one extraordinary moment, which I refer to as a touch from the Lord, The Blessed Cardinal Newman wrote this very moving hymn. It will definitely minister to all those who are facing loneliness, darkness and confusion.

On Saturday 18th September 2010, we attended the much needed Prayer Vigil at Hyde Park, London. Between 8 pm and 8-30 pm, darkness had fallen and most of the 80,000 congregants assembled were on their knees, heads bowed in solemn prayer on the grass in Hyde Park. In devotion and reverence this Hymn was sung, "Lead Kindly Light".

Visitors from all over the world have seen the video of this hymn. This will be an anointed final Hymn to sing in all churches when we finally depart, smartly dressed and in our coffins to be truly and warmly welcomed by our Beloved Saviour, Our Lord Jesus Christ of Nazareth into the glorious splendour of Heaven.

## Church Life

The Nicholas family worshipped at All Saints Church, Peckham for 13 years. From 20th July 2003 till the 23rd May 2010 the presiding Vicar was Rev. Frog Orr-Ewing.

For some time Anton led an early morning prayer meeting at St. Marks Chambers. During this period the Vicar was Nicholas Rivett-Carnac.

At the Memorial and Thanksgiving service for Nicholas Rivett-Carnac held at St. Marks Church on 23rd of October 2004 we heard Bishop Peter Walker... saying in his testimonial that the wife of the Rear Admiral told the Bishop that if her husband had just made one mistake, it would have changed the whole direction of the war.

Nicholas Rivett-Carnac joined the Scots Guards. He spent 10 years with the regiment, serving as a platoon commander during the fierce fighting in Malaya, when he was mentioned in dispatches. He returned home in time to take part in the Coronation Procession in 1953.
