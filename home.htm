1918–1920 · MALAYA

The first Asian banker of British Malaya

It was my father's Dad, Bastianpillai Paul Nicholas, who between the years of 1918–1920 commenced his first monetary business providing financial services in Ipoh, Perak, Malaya. Due to the date references he is recognised as one of the first Asian bankers in the history of British Colonial Malaya.

An all consumingly passionate entrepreneur driven by a strategic vision, who single-handedly brought an idea to pass, overcoming obdurate hurdles and colonial prepotency.

My Dad (Albert Arasaratnam Nicholas) is positioned in the centre of many family photographs. He was one of the Directors and the Manager of Oriental Bank of Malaya Ltd-Klang.

In 1936 a Colonial Licence was obtained by the visionary B. P. Nicholas under the illustrious title of Bank of Malaya Ltd.

HYMN · HYDE PARK, 2010

Lead, Kindly Light: sung by 80,000 kneeling in the darkness

John Henry Newman, who was a young English Priest, wrote the Hymn Lead Kindly Light when he was afflicted with a serious illness whilst staying on the island of Sicily — far from home, believing he was facing death. In one extraordinary moment, a touch from the Lord, the Blessed Cardinal Newman wrote this very moving hymn.

It will definitely minister to all those who are facing loneliness, darkness and confusion — and will be an anointed final hymn when we finally depart, smartly dressed and in our coffins to be truly and warmly welcomed by our Beloved Saviour, Our Lord Jesus Christ of Nazareth into the glorious splendour of Heaven.

On Saturday 18th September 2010 we attended the much needed Prayer Vigil at Hyde Park, London. Between 8 pm and 8.30 pm, darkness had fallen and most of the 80,000 congregants assembled were on their knees, heads bowed in solemn prayer on the grass in Hyde Park. In devotion and reverence this Hymn was sung, “Lead Kindly Light”.

CHURCH LIFE

Thirteen years at All Saints, Peckham

The Nicholas family worshipped at All Saints Church, Peckham for 13 years. From 20th July 2003 till the 23rd May 2010 the presiding Vicar was Rev. Frog Orr-Ewing.

For some time Anton led an early morning prayer meeting at St. Marks Chambers. During this period the Vicar was Nicholas Rivett-Carnac.

Nicholas Rivett-Carnac joined the Scots Guards. He spent 10 years with the regiment, serving as a platoon commander during the fierce fighting in Malaya, when he was mentioned in dispatches. He returned home in time to take part in the Coronation Procession in 1953.

His father ended his naval career as a Vice-Admiral and was involved in both World Wars, 1 and 2. He commanded the battleship Rodney and was Rear-Admiral in charge of the Normandy Beaches during the Allied invasion of Europe in 1944.

At the Memorial and Thanksgiving service for Nicholas Rivett-Carnac held at St. Marks Church on 23rd of October 2004 we heard Bishop Peter Walker, a former Principal of Westcott House Theological College Cambridge, saying in his testimonial that the wife of the Rear Admiral told the Bishop that if her husband had just made one mistake, it would have changed the whole direction of the war.
